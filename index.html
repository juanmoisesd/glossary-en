Aging Index

Formula: Aging Index = (Population 65+ / Population 0-14) × 100

A demographic indicator that measures the ratio of the elderly population (65+) to the young population (0-14), expressed per 100 young people. Values above 100 indicate that the elderly population exceeds the young population, signaling an advanced stage of demographic aging.

A value of 50 means there are 50 elderly persons for every 100 young people. Values above 100 indicate demographic inversion — more elderly than young.

Context: Latin America showed average aging index values of 25-30 in 2000, rising to 45-60 by 2023, reflecting the rapid demographic transition underway in the region.

  • PAHO Core Indicators
  • ECLAC Statistics

Dependency Ratio

Formula: Dependency Ratio = ((Population 0-14 + Population 65+) / Population 15-64) × 100

The ratio of the dependent population (those too young or too old to work: ages 0-14 and 65+) to the working-age population (15-64), expressed per 100 working-age adults. It measures the economic burden placed on the productive population.

A ratio of 50 means 50 dependents per 100 working-age persons. High values strain social security, healthcare, and pension systems.

Context: Latin American countries show dependency ratios declining from 70-80 in 2000 to 50-65 in 2023 as younger cohorts age into the workforce, though this dividend window is narrowing.

Primary sources:

  • World Bank Open Data
  • PAHO Core Indicators
  • ECLAC Demographics

NCRA Risk Score

Formula: NCRA Risk Score = f(aging_index, dependency_ratio, demographic_dividend_index, ...) [see methodology]

The Neuroeconomic Cognitive Risk Assessment (NCRA) Risk Score is a composite indicator (0-100) developed by de la Serna Tuya that integrates demographic, psychological, and economic variables to assess population-level vulnerability in cognitive and economic decision-making. Higher scores indicate greater risk.

Scores 0-30: Low risk. 31-60: Moderate risk. 61-80: High risk. 81-100: Critical risk. The score is not diagnostic at individual level — it is a population-level indicator.

Context: Developed as part of the NCRA Framework (V13). Applied to 11 Latin American countries (2000-2023). First application of neuroeconomic risk assessment to population-level demographic data.

Primary sources:

  • de la Serna Tuya (2024). NCRA LatAm Dataset v1.0. Zenodo.
  • NCRA Methodology Document
